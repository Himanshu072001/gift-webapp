My dearest Ankita,

Happy Birthday to the girl who walked into a coffee shop one nervous afternoon and somehow walked right into my entire life. I still don't think you fully realise what you did that day.

The 3rd of May, 2025. Both of us nervous, both of us pretending we weren't. That one cup of coffee started video calls that went on for hours, conversations that felt like home, and a feeling I couldn't quite name but didn't want to let go of.

Then life paused — the way life sometimes does. But I've always believed that God has a plan, and when nothing made sense, I held on to that faith — and it brought us back to each other on the 28th of September. I truly believe He had a hand in this. Because some things are just too perfectly timed to be accidental.

And then the 9th of March — our roka — the day I could say in front of everyone: she's the one. She was always the one. Now we wait for the day we get married, and honestly? I'd wait forever. Though I'd prefer sooner. 😄

Ankita, the way you make me feel at home — not in a house, but in a feeling — that is something no one else has ever given me. Your smile, the way you laugh at things that probably shouldn't be that funny, the warmth you carry everywhere you go — I am so lucky that nervous coffee shop date didn't go wrong.

On your birthday, I don't just want to wish you happiness. I want to promise you every ordinary evening, every cold coffee refill, every scoop of ice cream you absolutely need right now — I'll be there for all of it. Especially the ice cream emergencies.
